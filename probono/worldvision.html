World Vision promo

Sponsors’ lives have to be transformed as well.

IN 2019 I was asked by World Vision Singapore to direct a video pro bono featuring Mediacorp celebrity Felicia Chin. Targeting Singaporeans, the purpose was to raise funds and awareness for the sponsorship of underprivileged children in developing countries such as Myanmar.




S H A R E        

Director's Statement

We want happiness & achievement but what we need is contentment & fulfilment.

CHARITIES ARE INHERENTLY challenging. They expect contribution for a worthy cause. Yet the problems they are trying to solve are neither imminent nor directly relatable to most. Singaporeans are not miserly. We just don’t wish to be guilt-tripped for a cause we don’t believe in. It’s easy for us not to care about the problems of underprivileged children in another country. Unless we make it our problem. Then we start to care.

Singaporeans, amongst our many virtues, are known for our inclination towards pragmatism and our desire for wealth. We rationalize our voluntary participation in the rat race, in the name of financial security. Our pursuit of abundance has now become a burden.

Materialism breeds the scarcity mind set. We feel we never have enough. The more we have, the less each additional item is worth to us. Not enough happiness in our lives. Not enough achievements on paper. We feel eternally insecure.

HAPPINESS DOES NOT equate contentment. Achievement does not equate fulfilment. One is an insatiable addiction for an eat-till-you-drop buffet. The other is the cultivated appreciation for a three course meal. A sense of enough. A sense of meaning.


“As long as poverty, injustice and gross inequality persist in our world, none of us can truly rest.”
– Nelson Mandela

This video is not just about sponsoring underprivileged children in Myanmar. That is the external problem. The what. This video is also about privileged Singaporeans finding contentment and fulfilment. This is the internal problem. The why. It’s an uphill task to persuade the audience to commit to the what if they don’t have a strong enough why.

It's not enough that the lives of the underprivileged children are transformed. The sponsors’ lives have to be transformed as well. This video will attempt to show that by giving to the underprivileged,

We get to end our scarcity.
We get to end our own insecurities.
