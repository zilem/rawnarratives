About Zi Lem

An advocate for a higher cause.

LEM IS AN award-winning Singaporean filmmaker who has written and directed films in US, UK and China since 2011. He and his talented collaborators focus on stories that ignite the human spirit. He is currently developing two screenplays: Sheepwolf chronicles the founding of Singapore's leading self-defence movement to empower women, and Garage Spaceman recounts the volunteer driven mission to send the first Singaporean to space.

Lem wrote and directed his debut feature Prescient in Niagara Falls in 2015, a Singapore-US co-production produced with John Hannon and Monty Python producer Mark Forstater starring Michael Piccirilli and Pamelyn Chee. It won the Special Jury Prize for WNY Feature at BIFF. It is currently distributed in US, Europe, Asia, Australia, Middle East and Africa, and available on Amazon, iTunes, Google Play and Microsoft Store.

Lem produced a short Better Place in Beijing with director Linq Yim starring Golden Horse Nominee Tian Yuan. He wrote and directed a short Altered Memories in London starring Alex Rendall, Nina Fog, Oris Erhuero and Rebecca Roberts. He made one of his early films He who once was with Tommaso Rossellini and Austin Jack Lynch as an art student in New York.

Lem studied Art & Architecture for his undergrad at The Cooper Union in New York under renowned architects Raimund Abraham and Peter Eisenman. He later studied Directing in Los Angeles under the legendary directing coach Judith Weston as well as Writing & Directing at the London Film Academy under David Pope and Oscar winning directors Asif Kapadia and Pawel Pawlikowski.

Lem was one of the nine volunteers who helped send the first Singaporean to space during its maiden launch. He also worked pro bono as a story strategist to raise awareness of the mission. He previously worked in the food, finance, consulting and fashion industries. Having lived in 10 cities across 3 continents, he developed the uncanny ability to get strangers to open up about their lives, and is always inspired to find causes worth standing for.

S H A R E        

Act 1 - Why Film?

My love affair with story.

I FIRST DISCOVERED film as a 25 year old art student in New York. It struck me then that film, the study of human drama in creative form, could be the ultimate medium of self-expression I was searching for.

Having careered in many fields, from architecture and engineering to fashion, food, finance and consulting, none has proved as rewarding as understanding and sculpting human behavior and emotions through storytelling.

I used to be obsessed with the American indie thinking man’s sci-fi; films that commonly explored a what-if situation based on a scientific concept.

But as I survived each debilitating crisis: a divorce, losing a loved one to cancer, I appreciate in real life, one of screenwriting’s major tenets - character transformation. I could no longer return to making pure fiction based on imaginary concepts.


I longed to touch, move and inspire people through drama that have real-life consequences.

SCREENWRITING, THE CONCEPTION of story and drama, has become for me a useful tool for understanding life and for creating authentic story identity.

Today, I find myself increasingly drawn to stories that ignite the human spirit. They become the unifying theme across all the stories I tell. It keeps me questioning my values, motivations and insecurities. By remaining true to what I stand for, I create only stories that resonate with my audience on a deep emotional level.


S H A R E        

Act 2 - True Stories.

Inherently transformational.

IF YOU EVER witness a spacecraft crash upon launch, belly violently ripped apart, mechanical guts exposed, the moment is undeniably horrific...

...and incredibly breathtaking as it is hauled skywards by a giant jellyfish balloon. The image of the lone spacecraft drifting towards the vacuous heavens is so surreal it feels like a scene out of the Pixar animation movie Up. Except...

...debris from the crash reveal communications and crashables that were meant to ensure the astronaut's safe return to Earth. Now you're overcome with a dreadful thought. Is the astronaut paralyzed? Or worse, dead on arrival?

This is a true story.

For two harrowing hours in the remote desert of Alice Springs, Australia, I along with others on the team to send the first Singaporean to space experience tragedy unfold before our very eyes.

Encountering a near death experience is nothing short of life changing.

SUCH MOMENTS DRIVE home the impact of true stories, reinforcing a gut feeling I’ve had for years - pure fiction rarely reaches this level of truth. There comes a point where I have to admit being tired of ripping off my favourite films and conforming to cookie-cutter ways of storytelling.

Renowned screenwriter William Goldman once quipped,

“A Hollywood blockbuster tells us the lie we want to believe.
An indie film tells us the truth we don’t want to.”

The beauty of true stories lies in the vagaries of life, where motives are driven by real-life complexities and human contradictions. A reflection of the morally ambiguous world we inhabit. And knowing my subjects intimately in real life ensures that they'll never appear out of character on the page. Which ironically is also the burden of true stories. Especially when the slavish pursuit of the truth compromises the spirit of the story.

True stories are transformational not for the truth it unearths.

But for the meaning it unveils.

I'm thankful to have struck narrative gold with two such stories that ignite the human spirit.


S H A R E        

Act 3 - Igniting the Human Spirit.

A commitment to being timeless.

EXCEPTIONAL FEATURE FILMS take on average 7 years to evolve from concept to distribution. For the writers, producers and director involved, the toil, grief and bliss in getting the film made is akin to surviving a marriage.

In committing such time and energy to nurture an artistic product and the relationships revolving around it, we as filmmakers want to be involved in films that we can look back decades from now and still be proud of. So that even if they don't become box office hits or win prestigious awards,

They may ignite social movements and become something that endures.

They become the proverbial "This story needs to be told." because of the complexity of the moral dilemmas they explore. Hence, the choice of source material is of utmost significance. And the perspective more so than style used to tell these true stories needs to be fresh and controversial.


DEVELOPING THAT FRESH controversial perspective means constantly pushing the boundaries of the material as a writer. Which often leads to a transformation within that is so cathartic, it compels me to share it with the world. Even if it means taking years to get the film made.

With a stroke of luck, the film gets financed. And inevitably, compromises have to be made. The artistic integrity of the film tested. Truly tested. During such moments, a story about the human spirit

Serves as the moral compass that keeps the project from being derailed.

A story about the human spirit aids the commitment of making art that approaches timelessness.


S H A R E        

The Birth of a Vision.

A.I., Money & Purpose.

ON 27 JULY 2019, I woke up with a vision, powerful and clear. My why has been deeply embedded in my psyche all these years. Waiting to be articulated. In fact, my mission - to move and inspire the everyman with meaningful stories that ignite the human spirit was my what. It wasn’t my why.

Several years ago, I overheard a CPA lament to my friend about how she’s been losing business over the years to online tax preparation software such as TurboTax. My friend thought favourably of the situation considering that the CPA was now free to pursue tasks that were strategy oriented instead of those that required rote work. The CPA, a human being, remained unconvinced that the ability to finally rid her life of machine-like tasks was a godsend.

That was the cusp of A.I.

IN THE NOT-TOO-DISTANT future, states may disburse a Universal Basic Income for all human beings so that those whose jobs are displaced by A.I. do not end up homeless. While this solves major socioeconomic issues, it generates others.

Where our identity, behaviour and mindset used to be defined by our profession, what happens to our identities when our jobs are usurped by identityless robots?

When Universal Basic Income provided by the state surpasses the threshold of subsistence, we may no longer have to work for money.

When we no longer have to work for money,
the value of money diminishes.

Then where does value lie?

In the old world, value lies in our labour.
In the new world, value lies in our purpose.

When we can no longer motivate human beings with money, we can only appeal to their sense of purpose. Likewise, time will no longer be money. Time will become a measure of purposeful activity.

Purpose, meaning, fulfillment and calling may well define the human condition in an A.I. prevalent world.


I FOUND MY life’s calling when I was 25 and a struggling art student in New York. But I failed to act. I got mired in my family’s ailing business. I did a two year corporate stint. I started a trading business. Ironically trading my calling to keep up with the Joneses. Until I saw a friend act on his calling when he started his fashion label. That got me acting on mine.

How many of us have yet to discover our life’s calling?

How many of us have discovered our life’s calling but like me, got sidetracked and decided to keep up with the Joneses?

27 July 2007 marked my last day in the corporate realm. In a world increasingly driven by A.I. and financial security provided by the state, what we lose in our jobs, we gain in our purpose.

A.I. inadvertently forces us to contemplate the meaning of life.

And exactly twelve years since that fateful day, the vision I woke up with, the reason I choose to tell stories about the human spirit, the drive that has all along been fuelling my why is

For all human beings to discover & act on their life’s calling.
