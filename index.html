About Zi Lem

Outsider stories inspire self-actualization

ZI R. LEM, an award-winning screenwriter-filmmaker living in Portugal is passionate about outsider stories that inspire self-actualization. He co-wrote the feature screenplay "Sheepwolf" with Arlene Bogna which is based on the true story of the founding of Asia's largest self-defence movement to protect and empower women. Lem was one of nine volunteers involved in the maiden launch of the World’s first volunteer-driven mission by Singapore to send a man to space, of which he is developing the true story into the feature screenplay "Garage Spacemen". In addition, Zi is authoring the personal development book “Gap Year Asian - Why escape Asia before university”, a socio-anthropological critique of Asian vs Western values based on his experience living in the Anglosphere and continental Europe. Lem is a graduate of the London Film Academy where he studied under Oscar winning directors Asif Kapadia and Pawel Pawlikowski.

Lem wrote and directed his debut feature "Prescient" in Niagara Falls in 2015, a Singapore-US co-production produced with John Hannon and Monty Python producer Mark Forstater starring Michael Piccirilli and Pamelyn Chee. It won the Special Jury Prize for WNY Feature at BIFF. It is currently distributed in US, Europe, Asia, Australia, Middle East and Africa, and available on Amazon, iTunes, Google Play and Microsoft Store.

Lem wrote and directed "Altered Memories" in London starring Alex Rendall, Nina Fog, Oris Erhuero and Rebecca Roberts. He produced "Better Place" in Beijing with director Linq Yim starring Golden Horse Nominee Tian Yuan.

Lem directed "Self II" for fashion label Misomber Nuan's Fall 2011 collection which premiered at the Paris Fashion Week. He shot one of his early films "He who once was" in New York with Tommaso Rossellini and Austin Jack Lynch on 16mm with a Bolex, and edited it on a Steenbeck.

Lem studied Art and Architecture for his undergrad at The Cooper Union in New York under renowned architects Raimund Abraham and Peter Eisenman. He later studied Directing in Los Angeles under the legendary directing coach Judith Weston.

Lem previously worked in the food, finance, consulting and fashion industries in 11 cities across 3 continents. His prodigious life experience helped develop his uncanny ability to create a safe space for strangers, allowing them to open up about their lives, and in turn for Lem to discover inspiring stories and causes worth standing for.

S H A R E        

Stories that explore Self-Actualization

Questioning norms. Inspiring purpose.

WE ARE LIVING in an age characterized by unhealthy levels of consumerism - a phenomenon common in thriving Asian societies, if not all developed nations. For us, based on Maslow's hierarchy of needs, once our survival needs are met, we either struggle with psychological or self-fulfillment needs.

Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs

In the struggle beyond survival, Asians in particular are preoccupied with pragmatic, achievement-related money problems. Whether the Asian mindset of saving for a rainy day or the Western one of spending to enjoy the here and now is unhealthy is entirely a matter of values and perspective.

But there’s no denying that the need to make more money so that we can consume more material goods breeds the gnawing itch of scarcity. It's an addiction that arises from a place of fear. A fear of inadequacy. Keeping up with the Joneses inevitably leads to being stuck in the rat race.


“We buy things we don’t need.
With money we don’t have.
To impress people we don’t like.”

- Fight Club

A COMMON THEME I explore in my films is self-esteem vs self-actualization. Because the issue that plagues modern consumerist societies is not one of survival. But one of self-esteem.

When we struggle with issues of self-esteem, we’re shackled by the status quo - the social norms that we subconsciously partake in perpetuating. We’re measuring ourselves against the standards of others.

When we struggle with issues of self-actualization, we’re actively questioning the status quo - disrupting those shackling social norms. We’re measuring ourselves against the standards of our own.

By asking ourselves what we stand for, we begin to operate at the level of self-actualization. We begin to ask questions like


Is my current pursuit of having more money and stuff purposeful?

My films are less concerned with survival struggles as they are with


Inspiring the everyman to aspire to self-actualization.

Raw Narratives focuses on stories that evolve from self-esteem to self-actualization; the peak of Maslow's pyramid.



Sheepwolf chronicles the founding of Singapore's leading self-defence movement to empower women. Garage Spacemen recounts the World’s first volunteer-driven mission to send a man to space.



EXCEPTIONAL FEATURE FILMS take on average 7 years to evolve from concept to distribution. For the writers, producers and director involved, the toil, grief and bliss in getting the film made is akin to surviving a marriage.

In committing such time and energy to nurture an artistic product and the relationships revolving around it, we as filmmakers want to be involved in films that we can look back decades from now and still be proud of. So that even if they don't become box office hits or win prestigious awards,


They may ignite social movements and become phenomena that endure.

In the course of making these films, there will be times when the artistic integrity of the films is tested. Truly tested. During such moments, the mission to inspire purpose serves as the moral compass that keeps the projects from being derailed.

Over the years, I've found myself less concerned with building a career as a working filmmaker than I am with using film as a means to question social norms, in order that current and future generations free themselves from mindless activity in an artificial economy increasingly dominated by A.I. and


Begin pursuing their higher humanistic purpose in life.
