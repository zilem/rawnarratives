About Zi

An advocate for a higher cause.

LEM IS AN award-winning filmmaker who has written and directed for the last 10 years in the US, UK, China and Singapore. He discovered his vision, his calling and his life’s work after spending years interviewing hundreds across the globe about their life stories. He and his talented collaborators develop films about true stories that explore compelling causes, making them potential contenders for the foreign language Oscars.

Lem is currently scripting two feature screenplays based on the true stories behind Asia's largest realistic self-defence movement, and the volunteer-driven endeavour to send a man to space in a garage spacecraft.

Lem studied Art & Architecture at The Cooper Union in NY, Writing & Directing in London and Producing at UCLA. He has worked in the food, finance, consulting and fashion industries, and currently works pro bono as a brand strategist for the space endeavour. Having lived in 10 countries across 4 continents, he developed the uncanny ability to get strangers to open up about their lives, and is always inspired to find causes worth standing for.

Here is his life story.

S H A R E        

Act 1 - Why Film?

My love affair with story.

I FIRST DISCOVERED film as a 25 year old art student in New York. It struck me then that film, the study of human drama in creative form, could be the ultimate medium of self-expression I was searching for.

Having careered in many fields, from architecture and engineering to fashion, food, finance and consulting, none has proved as rewarding as understanding and sculpting human behavior and emotions through storytelling.

I used to be obsessed with the American indie thinking man’s Sci-Fi; films that commonly explored a what-if situation based on a scientific concept.

But as I survived each debilitating crisis: a divorce, losing a partner to cancer, I appreciate in real life, one of screenwriting’s major tenets - character transformation. I could no longer return to making pure fiction based on imaginary concepts.


I longed to touch, move and inspire people through drama that have real-life consequences.

SCREENWRITING, THE CONCEPTION of story and drama, has became for me a useful tool for understanding life and for creating authentic brand identity.

Today, I find myself increasingly drawn to true stories with compelling causes. They become the unifying theme across all the stories I tell. It keeps me questioning my values, my motivations and my insecurities. By remaining true to what I stand for, I create only stories that resonate with my audience on a deep emotional level.


S H A R E        

Act 2 - True Stories.

Inherently transformational.

IF YOU EVER witness a spacecraft crash upon launch, belly violently ripped apart, mechanical guts exposed, the moment is undeniably horrific...

...and incredibly breathtaking as it is hauled skywards by a giant jellyfish balloon. The image of the lone spacecraft drifting towards the vacuous heavens is so surreal it feels like a scene out of the Pixar animation movie Up. Except...

...debris from the crash reveal communications and crashables that were meant to ensure the astronaut's safe return to Earth. Now you're overcome with a dreadful thought. Is the astronaut paralyzed? Or worse, dead on arrival?

This is a true story.

For two harrowing hours in the remote desert of Alice Springs, Australia, I along with others on the team to send the first Singaporean to space experience tragedy unfold before our very eyes.

Encountering a near death experience is nothing short of life changing.

SUCH MOMENTS DRIVE home the impact of true stories, reinforcing a gut feeling I’ve had for years - pure fiction rarely reaches this level of truth. There comes a point where I have to admit being tired of ripping off my favourite films and conforming to cookie-cutter ways of storytelling.

Renowned screenwriter William Goldman once quipped,

“A Hollywood blockbuster tells us the lie we want to believe.
An indie film tells us the truth we don’t want to.”

The beauty of true stories lies in the vagaries of life, where motives are driven by real-life complexities and human contradictions. A reflection of the morally ambiguous world we inhabit. And knowing my subjects intimately in real life ensures that they'll never appear out of character on the page. Which ironically is also the burden of true stories. Especially when the slavish pursuit of the truth compromises the spirit of the story.

True stories are transformational not for the truth it unearths.

But for the meaning it unveils.

I'm thankful to have struck narrative gold with two such stories of compelling causes.


S H A R E        

Act 3 - Compelling Causes.

A commitment to being timeless.

EXCEPTIONAL FEATURE FILMS take on average 7 years to evolve from concept to distribution. For the writers, producers and director involved, the toil, grief and bliss in getting the film made is akin to surviving a marriage.

In committing such time and energy to nurture an artistic product and the relationships revolving around it, we as filmmakers want to be involved in films that we can look back decades from now and still be proud of. So that even if they don't become box office hits or win prestigious awards,

They may ignite social movements and become something that endures.

They become the proverbial "This story needs to be told." because of the complexity of the moral dilemmas they explore. Hence, the choice of source material is of utmost significance. And the perspective more so than style used to tell these true stories needs to be fresh and controversial.


DEVELOPING THAT FRESH controversial perspective means constantly pushing the boundaries of the material as a writer. Which often leads to a transformation within that is so cathartic, it compels me to share it with the world. Even if it means taking years to get the film made.

With a stroke of luck, the film gets financed. And inevitably, compromises have to be made. The artistic integrity of the film tested. Truly tested. During such moments,

A compelling cause serves as the moral compass that keeps the project from being derailed.

A compelling cause aids the commitment of making art that approaches timelessness.


S H A R E        

The Birth of a Vision.

A.I., Money & Purpose.

ON 27 JULY 2019, I woke up with a vision, powerful and clear. My why has been deeply embedded in my psyche all these years. Waiting to be articulated. In fact, my mission - to move and inspire audiences with stories about compelling causes was my what. It wasn’t my why.

Several years ago, I overheard a CPA lament to my friend about how she’s been losing business over the years to online tax preparation software such as TurboTax. My friend thought favourably of the situation considering that the CPA was now free to pursue tasks that were strategy oriented instead of those that required rote work. The CPA, a human being, remained unconvinced that the ability to finally rid her life of machine-like tasks was a godsend.

That was the cusp of A.I.

I LOVE A.I. because it’s usurping jobs that once belonged to lawyers, bankers, general practitioners, accountants, insurance agents, etc. Professionals that are relatively well compensated to perform unstimulating chores that consume little time. Basically, high ROI professions.

On the other hand, professionals who churn out creative, non cookie-cutter solutions have been languishing in financial misery. These are your designers, musicians, writers and architects. Tasks that require an inordinate amount of time to complete. Yet they yield comparatively meager financial rewards. Essentially, low ROI professions.

While A.I. may widen the income inequality between the rich and the poor,

A.I. levels the playing field between creatives and non-creatives.

So long as income disparity is tempered across professions, we are less likely to conform to societal pressures and seek out high paying jobs that we don't inherently desire. We are less likely to sell out.

This is especially so for high cost-of-living Asian societies that place a premium on pragmatism. Pragmatism is that oppressive life cycle of

Getting an education for the purpose of work.
Working unfulfilling jobs for the purpose of money.
Money for the purpose of keeping up with the Joneses.

Are we destined to spend half of our lives as indentured servants to corporations?

This was the existential question I struggled with up till my final day as a corporate animal. It simply didn't make sense that in the 21st century, our time and our freedom could be bought. Yet it was.

They used to say time is money. They won’t be saying it anymore in the not-too-distant future.


IN THE NOT-TOO-DISTANT future, the following may happen. The implementation of a robot tax as suggested by Bill Gates, where savings gained from A.I. can be redistributed to empathetic causes. The disbursement of a Universal Basic Income for all human beings so that those whose jobs are displaced by A.I. do not end up homeless. While this solves major socioeconomic issues, it generates others.

Where our identity, behaviour and mindset used to be defined by our profession, what happens to our identities when our jobs are usurped by identityless robots?

When Universal Basic Income provided by the state surpasses the threshold of subsistence, we no longer have to work for money.

When we no longer have to work for money,
the value of money diminishes.

Then where does value lie?

In the old world, value lies in our labour.
In the new world, value lies in our purpose.

When we can no longer motivate human beings with money, we can only appeal to their sense of purpose. Likewise, time will no longer be money. Time will become a measure of purposeful activity.

Purpose, meaning, fulfillment and calling are what defines the human condition in an A.I. prevalent world.


I’M A LATE bloomer. I found my life’s calling, film, when I was 25 and a struggling art student in New York. But I failed to act. I got mired in my family’s ailing business. I did a two year corporate stint. I started a trading business. Ironically trading my calling to keep up with the Joneses. Until I saw a friend act on his calling when he started his fashion label. That got me acting on mine.

How many of us have yet to discover our life’s calling?

How many of us have discovered our life’s calling but like me, got sidetracked and decided to keep up with the Joneses?

How many of us will continue spending 50% of our lives toiling in unfulfilling jobs instead of discovering and pursuing our life's calling?

27 July 2007 marked my last day in the corporate realm. In a world increasingly driven by A.I. and financial security provided by the state, what we lose in our jobs, we gain in our purpose.

A.I. inadvertently forces us to contemplate the meaning of life.

The reason I choose to tell stories about compelling causes is

To incite all human beings to act on their life’s calling.

And exactly twelve years since that fateful day, I’ve come full circle to realizing that this has all along been my why.
