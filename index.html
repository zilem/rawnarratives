About Zi Lem

Breaking common ground with the uncommon

LEM IS AN award-winning Singaporean filmmaker who has written and directed films in US, UK and China since 2010. He and his talented collaborators focus on stories that ignite the human spirit. He is currently developing two screenplays: Sheepwolf chronicles the founding of Singapore's leading self-defence movement to empower women, and Garage Spacemen recounts the World’s first volunteer-driven mission to send a man to space.

Lem wrote and directed his debut feature Prescient in Niagara Falls in 2015, a Singapore-US co-production produced with John Hannon and Monty Python producer Mark Forstater starring Michael Piccirilli and Pamelyn Chee. It won the Special Jury Prize for WNY Feature at BIFF. It is currently distributed in US, Europe, Asia, Australia, Middle East and Africa, and available on Amazon, iTunes, Google Play and Microsoft Store.

Lem wrote and directed Altered Memories in London starring Alex Rendall, Nina Fog, Oris Erhuero and Rebecca Roberts. He produced Better Place in Beijing with director Linq Yim starring Golden Horse Nominee Tian Yuan.

Lem directed Self II for fashion label Misomber Nuan's Fall 2011 collection which premiered at the Paris Fashion Week. He shot one of his early films He who once was in New York with Tommaso Rossellini and Austin Jack Lynch on 16mm with a Bolex, and edited it on a Steenbeck.

Lem studied Art & Architecture for his undergrad at The Cooper Union in New York under renowned architects Raimund Abraham and Peter Eisenman. He later studied Directing in Los Angeles under the legendary directing coach Judith Weston as well as Writing & Directing at the London Film Academy under David Pope and Oscar winning directors Asif Kapadia and Pawel Pawlikowski.

Lem was one of the nine volunteers behind the maiden launch of the World’s first volunteer-driven mission to send a man to space. He also worked pro bono as a story strategist to raise awareness of the mission. He previously worked in the food, finance, consulting and fashion industries. Having lived in 10 cities across 3 continents, he developed the uncanny ability to get strangers to open up about their lives, and is always inspired to find causes worth standing for.

Films about Self-Actualization

Inspiring Purpose

MASLOW’S HIERARCHY OF needs has three broad categories. At the bottom, human beings struggle with survival needs. Once survival needs are fulfilled, they either struggle with psychological needs or self-fulfillment needs.

Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs, Raw Narratives logo.

For a significant part of my life, I have struggled with the obsession with materialism commonly observed in Asian societies, if not, all developed nations around the world. Keeping up with the Joneses inevitably leads to being stuck in the miserable rat race. In other words, struggling with psychological needs or that of self-esteem.


“We buy things we don’t need.
With money we don’t have.
To impress people we don’t like.”

- Fight Club

A common theme I explore in my films is that of self-esteem vs self-actualization. Because the issue that plagues modern consumerist societies is not that of survival. But that of self-esteem.

Therefore, my stories are less concerned with survival struggles as they are with


Inspiring the everyman to aspire to self-actualization.

And my approach towards filmmaking is less about building a career as a working filmmaker than it is about using film as a vehicle to effect social change.

I believe that in order for my generation and future generations to extricate ourselves from mindless economic activity, we have to begin questioning and pursuing our purpose in life.


S H A R E        

Birth of a Vision

A.I., Money & Purpose

A CPA ONCE lamented to my friend about how her business was adversely affected by the rise of online tax preparation software such as TurboTax. My friend thought favourably of the situation considering that the CPA was now free to pursue tasks that were strategy oriented instead of those that required rote work. The CPA, a human being, remained unconvinced that the ability to finally rid her life of machine-like tasks was a godsend.


That was the cusp of A.I.

In the not-too-distant future, states may disburse a Universal Basic Income for all human beings so that those whose jobs are displaced by A.I. do not end up homeless. While this solves major socioeconomic issues, it generates others.

Where our identity, behaviour and mindset used to be defined by our profession, what happens to our identities when our jobs are usurped by identityless robots?

When Universal Basic Income provided by the state surpasses the threshold of subsistence, we may no longer have to work for money.


When we no longer work for money,
the value of money diminishes.

When we no longer work, what happens to our role as providers? What happens to our self-esteem? More importantly, where does value lie?


In the old world, value lies in our labour.
In the new world, value lies in our purpose.

When we can no longer motivate human beings with money, we can only appeal to their sense of purpose. Likewise, time will no longer be money. Time will become a measure of purposeful activity.

Purpose, meaning, fulfillment and calling may well define the human condition in an A.I. prevalent world.



I FOUND MY life’s calling when I was 25 and an aspiring filmmaker in New York. But I failed to act. I got mired in my family’s ailing business. I did a two year corporate stint. I started a trading business. Ironically trading my calling to keep up with the Joneses. Until I saw a friend act on his calling when he started his fashion label. That got me acting on mine.

How many of us have yet to discover our life’s calling?

How many of us have discovered our life’s calling but like me, got sidetracked and decided to keep up with the Joneses?

27 July 2007 marked my last day in the corporate realm. In a world increasingly driven by A.I. and financial security provided by the state, what we lose in our jobs, we gain in our purpose.


A.I. inadvertently forces us to contemplate our purpose in life.

Exactly twelve years since that fateful day, the vision I woke up with, the reason I choose to tell stories about the human spirit, the drive that has all along been fuelling my why is


For all human beings to discover & act on their life’s calling.
