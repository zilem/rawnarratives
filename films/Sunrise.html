Nocturnal Sunrise

A love story about guilt & the burden of masculinity

A NOCTURNAL MAN begins a love affair with a married socialite until a terminal illness befalls her and he is forced to confront the mundane existence of humanity.


S H A R E        

Synopsis

A tale about existential crises

DURING A NIGHT of passionless lovemaking, a nocturnal man and his wife repeats the same old fight. She wants kids. He doesn’t. Ever since putting his artist career in Singapore on hold to engage in financial trading yet failing to yield a sustainable income, he is unwilling to focus on his art


Out of a masculine need to prove that he can provide financially.

As his trading losses mount, he grows increasingly pessimistic about life (in Singapore). And by extension, bringing life into this world. They eventually agree to a separation.

Shortly after, he reconnects with an old friend who also suffers from a broken marriage. The two confide in their shared pain, igniting dormant feelings they have had for one another over the years. As lust fans the flames of their passion, she becomes pregnant with his child, throwing a spanner in the works. Before they make a decision about whether or not to keep the child, she suffers a miscarriage.

Oddly enough, he isn't surprised. He reveals that he belongs to a nocturnal species. Which meant the offspring of a human-nocturnal union rarely survives. While capable of outliving humans by several life times,


Nocturnals suffer from a blood disorder that decimates them when exposed to the sun.



AS SHE RECOVERS from her miscarriage, she learns of an even worse predicament - her cancer has relapsed. Only this time, it’s terminal. When her condition worsens and hospital stays become more frequent, she requests him to stay by her side at the hospital more often. Even during periods when the husband is absent, he is hesitant to be an outwardly caring lover.


The more time he spends with her, the more guilt he develops toward her husband.

Believing that his blood may have the ability to save her, he performs an unorthodox blood transfusion. This appears to alleviate her symptoms temporarily but otherwise, has no impact on her recovery.

After an interminably long hospital stay in which she fails to check in with him, he becomes distressed. Upon visiting her, he realizes to his horror that she no longer recognizes him. The cancer has spread so widely that part of her brain has shut down. Now, he truly regrets not spending more time with her prior. Unfortunately, it’s too little too late.

A few weeks later, she goes blind. A few days after that, she passes. On her deathbed, he decides to fulfill an unfulfilled request of hers -


To see the sunrise together.


S H A R E        

Director's Statement

An addiction to morals, pragmatism & responsibility

BEYOND A LOVE story, this is a tale about the invisible societal forces that shape our gender roles and that prevents us from self-actualizing. Some of the themes explored are the Asian male as default provider and the role of pragmatism in Asian societies like Singapore.


Singapore is treated as a character in the story.

Nocturnal Sunrise is inspired by the true story of a Singaporean male artist who struggles with his masculine instinct of not depending on his female partner during times of financial duress. He gives up his art to focus his energies on financial trading. To him, time not spent generating income or thinking about generating income is a sign that he isn’t fulfilling his responsibilities as a man.

Whether such burden of responsibility is self-imposed or a result of living in Singapore is arguable. He becomes caught up in proving his own self worth via achievements in dollars and cents rather than the fulfillment he obtains from his art. In the end, one thing is clear.


He fails to self-actualize.


THE NOCTURNAL MAN as protagonist stems from my fascination with broken heroes. A hero with supernatural abilities that far from empowering him, burden, trap and imprison him in a state of moral, mental and emotional brokenness.

The nocturnal man suffers from an evolutionary blood disorder that allows him to live many human life times. Yet his life experiences are limited to half a day every day because exposure to sunlight destroys him. In a way, he is half a human. So long as he stays within his bubble, he gets to live out a sheltered, mundane existence.


The nocturnal man is a metaphor for life in Singapore.


THE MORAL CONUNDRUM of infidelity in the face of a terminal illness was what fascinated me most about this story. The guilt the male lover experiences toward the husband of the wife he’s having an affair with. The greater his love for his lover, the greater his guilt toward the husband. Yet how can he withhold love from a dying person?

His moral dilemma stems from wanting to be a caring lover. As well as a righteous man.

He can be either. But he can’t be both. He leans toward being righteous only to regret when circumstances are too dire too late. But who’s to say there wouldn’t be worst repercussions if he acted less righteously?

There is no moral to this story. I have tried my best to withhold judgments on the characters involved. This is intentional. I wrote this story primarily for audiences to live vicariously through the characters involved in this knotty situation, so that they may form their own conclusions and inform their own choices about the future.
