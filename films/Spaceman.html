Garage Spaceman

The attempt is more significant than the outcome.

THE INCREDIBLE TRUE story of a maverick scientist who led a team of nine volunteers in a death-defying mission to send the first Singaporean to space in a garage spacecraft.

Following the launch on 31 May 2019, the screenplay is currently in development, slated for production in 2021.


S H A R E        

Synopsis

An incredible mission turns awry.

DURING A LIVE TV interview, former defense scientist Lim Seng announces that his team of volunteers will send the first Singaporean to space from Alice Springs, Australia on 15th May 2018. Chosen from over a hundred pilot candidates, the astronaut will ascend to the Armstrong line 20km above Earth in a space capsule with the help of a stratospheric balloon. With many careers on the line,


The astronaut’s identity remains a secret until launch.

In the days leading up to the launch, over thirty volunteers comprising mainly pilots and engineers gather at Alice Springs. The astronaut is revealed to be Major Yip, a F15 commander with the Air Force, also known by his call sign "Smash". Unforeseen violent upper winds emerge, with the possibility of rupturing the balloon if they proceed with launching.


Regrettably, the mission is postponed until the following year.


A YEAR LATER, the volunteers descend on Alice Springs a few days before the second launch attempt on 26 April 2019. Despite being under twenty strong, the team is ready well before the designated date. The weather turns out perfect two days prior. Unfortunately, a mandatory GPS device required by aviation authorities malfunctions and by the time the engineers resolve the issue,


The window for a safe launch has once again passed.

While the rest of the team head back to Singapore, Lim Seng and a few engineers stay behind to prep for a third launch attempt on 7 May 2019. With the team down to a handful, the pilots are concerned that Lim Seng’s eagerness to complete the mission may result in a compromise of safety. Without favorable weather conditions in the foreseeable future and job commitments to attend to, Smash returns to Singapore.


No pilot. No astronaut. No mission.

As Lim Seng mulls over this new reality, he recognizes that part of the pilots’ insistence for perfect weather conditions stem from their exposure to high safety standards.

Despite spending years determining the right candidate, he realizes he would be guilt-stricken for life should anything untoward happen to Smash during the mission. As the engineer who built the system from scratch,


Lim Seng decides to don the space suit. To become the astronaut.

Identifying a window for launch, Lim Seng wastes no time in rallying his troops for a fourth launch attempt on 31 May 2019. This new team, ten strong, would mostly be new inexperienced non-engineers. And no pilots. But this would do. After all, beggars can't be choosers.




ON THE DAY of the launch, many blunders occur due to the team’s unfamiliarity with the process. But none prove particularly detrimental. Except for a new safety procedure introduced a few hours before launch. This one minor modification would become the straw that breaks the camel's back; setting into motion a chain of calamitous events.

Starting with the capsule crashing upon launch, its belly gets violently ripped apart, spilling its mechanical guts in its wake. The debris reveal communications equipment and crashables that ensure a safe landing. Without a way to reach Lim Seng,


The objective is no longer the Armstrong line.

But to get Lim Seng safely back to Earth.

Once the capsule clears the mountains at 8km, the parachute is deployed. Then search and rescue initiated. For two harrowing hours, the team is fraught with the dreadful thought of Lim Seng either paralyzed, or worse, dead on arrival.

When Lim Seng is eventually found, it turns out the initial crash comprised both the integrity of the capsule as well as the space suit. Had he ascend any higher, he would probably perish from the lack of oxygen in the upper atmosphere. But luckily for Lim Seng, he survives the ordeal to tell his remarkable journey.

Rebels & Patriots

Innovation is by nature non-conforming.

IN 2019, ANTICIPATING the launch on 26 April 2019, we created the following video to reach out to our intended audience - Rebels & Patriots.

Current rebels include pilots, makers, inventors, entrepreneurs, TED organizers and artists. Potential rebels are risk-takers who associate themselves with our ideals.

Patriots love their country. They want to see it thrive. To thrive is to evolve. To evolve, a nation must persevere. To persevere, the individual must take initiative.

The GoSpace project is not just about bringing a man in a capsule to the sky and back to Earth. That is purely an engineering feat. That is the external problem. The what. The GoSpace project is also about the need for Singaporeans to evolve. This is the internal problem. The why.

People don’t buy our what if they haven’t got a clue our why.
