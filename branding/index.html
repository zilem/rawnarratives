BRANDING

Giving your Audience a compelling Identity they can't resist.

I BELIEVE THERE are two components to branding and a third that makes it extremely successful.

1.

Defining your brand's authentic identity.


2.

Addressing why your intended audience needs your product.

Most agencies reverse engineer an incomplete understanding of your brand identity based on a vague idea of your target audience. This is unsustainable in the long run due to a lack of self-awareness.

Who am I?

My forte lies in helping others understand what makes them tick. I distill the essence of what defines you. What you stand for? For most startup founders, the answer lies on a very sub-conscious level. Without clarity to their own cause, they fail to stir & arouse a deeper emotional connection with their audience.


Branding is defining your brand's authentic identity in a way that addresses why your intended audience needs your product.

My definition of branding is unconcerned with having a Unique Selling Proposition.


Being unique is the outcome.
It should never be the objective.

This begins with asking why your brand exists. Its raison d’être. Not what your brand sells.

Your brand’s reason for existence should speak to your own insecurities on some level. This allows you to understand the insecurities of your intended audience. This is profound. Because now you know why they need your product. Not simply what product they are looking for.


Branding is futile when a client doesn’t have a strong enough why and are merely contented with selling a commonplace what.


DEVELOPING A SUCCESSFUL brand is primarily about storytelling. Though many fail to ask who the story is for and more importantly, what do they want.


A well told story is an exercise in empathy.

1. It acts as an excellent PR & marketing vehicle for customers and investors.

2. It guides the mission, vision, principles, culture and business plan.


Most startup founders underestimate the impact a well told story has on their brand's direction, potential & influence.


OF ALL THE storytelling professions, the screenwriter is the only one whose role is to draft the blueprint of a multimillion-dollar product designed to entertain its audience, appeal to human emotions and possibly promote a cause.

As a screenwriter-filmmaker, I apply these techniques to branding. We begin with a conversation about your personal journey specific to your brand. I’m seeking goals, villains, stakes and triumphs that your intended audience can identify with as part of their common struggle.

Somewhere in that mutual exploration, we may discover that your brand speaks to a higher cause.


In serving the higher cause,
a brand transcends itself to become a movement.

The third component that makes a brand extremely successful is when your movement is so compelling that your intended audience can’t resist deriving their own identity from your cause. Think Apple and Virgin.


Past the tipping point, your brand becomes legend.
