raw Story Strategy

Building an authentic narrative that appeals to your audience

DEVELOPING A SUCCESSFUL brand these days is primarily about storytelling. Story strategy is all about creating a strong brand identity. There are two components that are essential for an excellent story strategy and a third that would make the brand phenomenal.

1.
Defining your brand's authentic identity.

2.
Addressing why your intended audience needs your product.

Without clarity of their own cause, startup founders often fail to stir & arouse a deeper emotional connection within their audience. Instead they worry about coming up with a Unique Selling Proposition.


Being unique is the outcome.

It should never be the objective.

My forte lies in helping startup founders clarify what makes them tick - identifying what they stand for as well as the recurring themes in their lives. These are the ingredients necessary to crafting an authentic narrative.



ESTABLISHING AN EMOTIONAL connection with your audience begins with asking why your brand exists. Its raison d’être. Not what your brand sells.

Your brand’s reason for existence should speak to your own insecurities on some level. This allows you to understand the insecurities of your intended audience.


Your brand's story is about selling your own insecurities.

This is profound. Because now you know why your audience needs your product. Not simply what product they are looking for. Here are a few examples.


“Will I be able to overcome my fear of computers and technology?”
Apple

“Will I be able to diagnose my health issues and afford preventive healthcare?”
Theranos

"Will I be stuck in the traditional medical system and never find a cure?"
Alternative Medicine

Branding is futile when a founder doesn’t have a strong enough why and is merely contented with selling a commonplace what.



MOST STARTUP FOUNDERS recognize the significance of a good story. What they fail to ask is who the story is meant for and what do their audiences want. Perhaps the most overlooked question is what do their audiences believe.


A well told story is an exercise in the art of empathy.

Empathy is the ability to shift into the perspective of others. It is not about pity, sympathy or compassion. It is the emotional sensitivity required in identifying with others that allows a well told story to...

1. Act as an excellent PR & marketing vehicle for customers and investors.

2. Guide the mission, vision, values and principles for employees.


Founders underestimate the impact a well told story has on their brand's direction, potential & influence.

OF ALL THE storytelling professions, the screenwriter's role and responsibility is to draft the blueprint of a multi-million dollar product designed to entertain its audience, appeal to emotions, and possibly promote a cause.

As a screenwriter-filmmaker, I apply these techniques to building your brand's identity via story. We begin with a conversation about your personal journey specific to your brand. I’m seeking goals, villains, stakes and triumphs that your intended audience can identify with as part of their common struggle.

As with crafting a screenplay built on emotionally resonant themes, your brand identity not only has to resonate deeply with its intended audience, it needs to be meticulously aligned from the big picture to the smallest detail, so as to ensure cohesive branding.

Somewhere in that mutual exploration, we may discover that your brand speaks to a higher cause.


In serving the higher cause,
a brand transcends itself to become a movement.

The third component of a story strategy that makes a brand phenomenal is when your movement becomes so compelling...


3.
Your audience can’t resist deriving their own identity from your cause.

Because now they have something to believe in. Think Apple, Tesla and the once aspiring Theranos. Past the tipping point, your brand becomes legend.
